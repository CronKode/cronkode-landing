PAIA Manual

Promotion of Access to Information Act 2 of 2000
Last updated: April 2026 · Version 1.0
CronKode (Pty) Ltd — Private Body

This manual is published in terms of Section 51 of the Promotion of Access to Information Act 2 of 2000 (PAIA). It describes the records held by CronKode (Pty) Ltd and how members of the public may request access to those records.

1. Introduction

The Promotion of Access to Information Act 2 of 2000 (PAIA) gives effect to the constitutional right of access to information held by both public and private bodies. CronKode (Pty) Ltd is committed to transparency and to facilitating access to information in accordance with PAIA and the Protection of Personal Information Act 4 of 2013 (POPIA).

This manual serves as a guide to the records held by CronKode (Pty) Ltd and the procedures for requesting access to such records.

6. How to Request Access to Records

Any person wishing to request access to records held by CronKode (Pty) Ltd must submit a written request to the Information Officer. The request must be submitted using Form C as prescribed under PAIA, or a substantially similar written request containing the following information:

  • Full name and contact details of the requester
  • Description of the record requested
  • The form in which access is required (copy, inspection, etc.)
  • Reason for the request (where applicable)
  • Proof of identity

Requests must be submitted to: hello@cronkode.co.za

9. Grounds for Refusal

CronKode (Pty) Ltd may refuse a request for access to records on the following grounds as permitted by PAIA:

  • The record contains personal information of a third party who has not consented
  • Disclosure would harm the commercial interests of CronKode (Pty) Ltd
  • The record is protected by legal privilege
  • The record contains confidential information supplied by a third party
  • Disclosure would be in breach of a legal obligation of confidentiality

10. Third Party Notification

Where a request concerns records that contain information about a third party, CronKode (Pty) Ltd will notify that third party and provide them with an opportunity to make representations before a decision is made.

11. Appeals and Complaints

If a requester is dissatisfied with a decision made by CronKode (Pty) Ltd regarding their PAIA request, they may:

  • Apply to a court for relief in terms of Section 78 of PAIA
  • Lodge a complaint with the Information Regulator of South Africa

Information Regulator contact details:

  • Website: inforegulator.org.za
  • Email: inforeg@justice.gov.za
  • Complaints email: POPIAComplaints@inforegulator.org.za

12. Data Subject Rights Under POPIA

In addition to PAIA rights, data subjects have the following rights under POPIA regarding their personal information held by CronKode (Pty) Ltd:

  • Right to access their personal information
  • Right to request correction of inaccurate information
  • Right to request deletion of their personal information
  • Right to object to processing of their personal information
  • Right to lodge a complaint with the Information Regulator

All such requests must be submitted to hello@cronkode.co.za and will be responded to within 30 days.

13. Updates to This Manual

This PAIA Manual will be reviewed and updated annually or whenever significant changes occur in the organisation's structure, operations or information holdings. The current version will always be available at cronkode.co.za/paia.
